# How Tech Companies Use Experiments to Make Billion-Dollar Decisions - A/B Testing Explained Intuitively

## 1. Why Opinions Are Dangerous

Open with the tension inside product teams: everyone has a confident idea, but users are the only source of truth.

## 2. The Power of Controlled Experiments

Explain control vs treatment as a fair comparison between the world we know and the world we want to test.

## 3. Understanding Randomness

Show why good and bad luck can make experiments look better or worse than reality.

## 4. Hypothesis Testing

Frame hypothesis testing as evidence-based decision-making, not academic ritual.

## 5. Conversion Rates

Explain conversion rates as the basic pulse of a product experiment.

## 6. Statistical Significance

Explain p-values as a guardrail against false excitement.

## 7. Confidence Intervals

Describe confidence intervals as uncertainty ranges that keep teams honest.

## 8. Lift and Business Impact

Translate percentage-point changes into incremental customers, revenue, and decision value.

## 9. Experiment Pitfalls

Cover peeking, underpowered tests, selection bias, novelty effects, seasonality, and multiple testing.

## 10. Real-World Applications

Connect A/B testing to ecommerce, onboarding, pricing, recommendations, ads, and growth systems.

## 11. Final Takeaway

Close with A/B testing as the discipline of replacing opinions with evidence.
